CASE STUDY

1970 Chevelle SS Build

Problem

The client brought in a shell that had been sitting in a field for 20 years. Major rust issues in the floor pans, quarters, and trunk. No drivetrain, interior stripped.

Solution

A full frame-off, rotisserie restoration. The goal wasn’t just to restore it, but to build a modern-driving muscle car with classic looks.

Process

Started with media blasting to assess the true damage. Replaced all sheet metal from the firewall back. Powder-coated the frame, installed modern QA1 suspension, and dropped in a Chevy Performance 572 big block.

Results

A 700+ horsepower show winner that drives beautifully on the street and tears up the track.

Tools/Parts Used

  • Chevy Performance 572ci Crate Engine
  • Tremec T-56 Magnum 6-Speed
  • QA1 Level 3 Handling Suspension Kit
  • Wilwood 4-Wheel Disc Brakes
  • Vintage Air Gen IV SureFit System
